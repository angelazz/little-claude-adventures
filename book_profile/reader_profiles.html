Your profile is determined by what you have chosen to read, not just how many books. The algorithm looks for the strongest signal across four axes — era, difficulty, theme, and breadth — and assigns the profile that best fits your reading. Your current profile is highlighted throughout.

Profiles are assigned by finding the strongest signal in your reading. Era preference fires first — if 35% or more of your reads fall in one period, that defines you. Difficulty fires next if your average is notably high (2.6+) or low (1.55 or below). Theme clusters fire if 30% or more of your reads share a clear sensibility. Breadth fires last — as Obsessive if one theme still quietly dominates, or Generalist if you have covered five or more distinct themes.

Fewer than 5 books read means no signal is strong enough yet, so a baseline profile applies. Check off more books and watch yours shift.
